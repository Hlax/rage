Effects of AI-Assisted Brainstorming on Idea Quality and Semantic Diversity
(Fixture: fake academic-style passage for golden tests.)

In a controlled study of short-form writing tasks, we examined how AI-assisted
brainstorming affects the ideas that participants submit. Participants were
undergraduate students recruited from introductory writing courses.

We found that AI-assisted brainstorming increased average idea quality across
submitted ideas. Independent raters scored ideas produced with AI assistance
higher on usefulness and coherence than ideas produced without assistance.

However, AI-assisted brainstorming reduced semantic diversity across submitted
ideas. Ideas generated with AI support clustered around fewer distinct semantic
directions, even when individual idea quality improved.

Limitations: the study only tested short-form writing tasks, and the sample
consisted of student participants. The results may not generalize to film,
music, or professional creative work, where task structure, expertise, and
incentives differ substantially.
